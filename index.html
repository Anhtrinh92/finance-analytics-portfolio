Key Insights

Premium subscription is the dominant revenue engine, contributing 70–93% of total revenue across the period — averaging ~88% — while Ad-supported revenue accounts for only 7–15%, remaining structurally secondary despite MAU growth.

Total MAUs grew from 131M (Q1 2017) to 515M (Q1 2023), but Premium ARPU declined from €5.46 to €4.32 over the same period — indicating that user growth has been driven by lower-priced plans (family, student, emerging markets), compressing per-user monetisation.

Gross margin is volatile and wide-ranging (11.6–40.2%), not stable as it might appear — recent quarters have settled around ~25%, reflecting ongoing pressure from Cost of Revenue (royalties & licensing) growing in line with revenue.

Despite revenue growth, Spotify recorded operating losses in all recent quarters — Q1 2023: −€146M, Q4 2022: −€231M — as R&D, Sales & Marketing, and G&A costs consistently outpace gross profit, making operational leverage the key path to profitability.

Ad-supported MAUs make up ~59% of total users but generate a fraction of revenue — Ad gross profit has been negative in multiple quarters, suggesting the ad business is not yet self-sustaining and cross-subsidised by Premium.
